# Phrases, Idioms & Storytelling

Useful phrases, collocations, idioms, and expressions for natural conversation and storytelling.

## Useful Phrases & Collocations

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **a great deal** | Noun phrase | Велика кількість, дуже багато | A large amount or number of something | She has learned a great deal since starting the course. |
| 2 | **based on** | Preposition phrase | На основі, базуючись на | Using something as a foundation or starting point | The film is based on a true story. |
| 3 | **hard to be sure** | Adjective phrase | Важко бути впевненим | Difficult to say with certainty | It's hard to be sure whether he understood the task. |
| 4 | **intend to** | Verb phrase | Мати намір, збиратися | To plan or mean to do something | I intend to study for two hours every evening. |
| 5 | **judging by** | Preposition phrase | Судячи з, виходячи з | Based on evidence or appearance | Judging by his marks, he studied very hard. |
| 6 | **put to good use** | Verb phrase | Використати з користю | To use something in a beneficial or productive way | She put her language skills to good use by becoming a translator. |
| 7 | **make eye contact** | Verb phrase | Встановлювати зоровий контакт | To look directly into someone's eyes | It's important to make eye contact when you're talking to someone. |
| 8 | **have an effect on** | Verb phrase | Мати вплив на, впливати | To cause a change in someone or something | His words had a profound effect on me. |
| 9 | **read body language** | Verb phrase | Читати мову тіла | To understand what someone is thinking by observing their gestures | She's good at reading people's body language. |

## Storytelling & Anecdotes

Useful phrases for telling stories and describing events in a natural, engaging way.

### Introducing the Story

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 17 | **(Right, so) I'm going to tell you about...** | Phrase | Отже, я розповім вам про... | Used to introduce a story in an informal, conversational way | Right, so I'm going to tell you about a great day out I had last summer. |
| 18 | **(OK, so) this is a story about...** | Phrase | Гаразд, це історія про... | Another informal way to begin telling an anecdote | OK, so this is a story about a day I'll never forget. |
| 19 | **This took place (about)...** | Phrase | Це сталося (приблизно)... | Used to say when something happened | This took place about a month ago when I was visiting my grandmother. |

### Sequencing Events

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 20 | **As soon as / When / Just after...** | Conjunction / Phrase | Як тільки / Коли / Одразу після того як... | Used to show that one event happened immediately after another | As soon as we got there, the sun came out. |
| 21 | **Initially / To begin with...** | Adverb / Phrase | Спочатку, на початку | Used to describe what happened at the start | Initially, we were the only people there, but that soon changed. |
| 22 | **As / While...** | Conjunction | Коли, в той час як | Used to describe two actions happening at the same time | As we were sitting there, more people started arriving. |
| 23 | **Suddenly / All of a sudden...** | Adverb / Phrase | Раптом, несподівано | Used to show that something happened quickly and unexpectedly | Suddenly, the weather changed and it started raining. |

### Describing Events Vividly

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 24 | **Predictably / As expected...** | Adverb / Phrase | Передбачувано, як і очікувалося | Used when something happened as you thought it would | Predictably, other people soon started arriving when the weather improved. |
| 25 | **Unexpectedly / Out of the blue...** | Adverb / Phrase | Несподівано, зненацька | Used when something happened that you didn't expect | Unexpectedly, it started pouring with rain even though the forecast said it would be sunny. |
| 26 | **Luckily / Fortunately...** | Adverb | На щастя, на вдачу | Used to say that something good happened by chance | Luckily, after it stopped raining, the sun came out again. |

### Finishing the Story

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 27 | **Without a doubt, it was...** | Phrase | Без сумніву, це було... | Used to emphasize that you are certain about something | Without a doubt, it was one of the best days I've had in ages. |
| 28 | **When I look back (on that day) now...** | Phrase | Коли я зараз озираюся назад (на той день)... | Used to reflect on past events from your current perspective | When I look back on that day now, I feel so grateful I went. |
| 29 | **It turned out to be...** | Phrase | Виявилося, що це було... | Used to describe how something ended or what the final result was | It turned out to be an unforgettable day out. |
| 30 | **Strangely / Funnily enough...** | Adverb phrase | Як не дивно, цікаво те що... | Used to introduce something surprising or ironic | Strangely enough, the best days are often the ones you don't really plan. |
