# Body Language & Actions

Verbs and phrases for describing physical reactions, gestures, and ways of looking.

## Physical Reactions & Gestures

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **crouch down** | Phrasal verb | Присісти, присідати навпочіпки | To lower your body close to the ground by bending your knees | She crouched down to pick up the coin from the floor. |
| 2 | **flicker across** | Phrasal verb | Промайнути (про вираз обличчя, емоцію) | To appear briefly on someone's face (used for emotions or expressions) | A smile flickered across her face when she heard the good news. |
| 3 | **flinch** | Verb | Здригнутися, відсмикнутися | To make a sudden small movement because of pain, fear, or surprise | He flinched when the doctor touched his injured arm. |
| 4 | **give a wave** | Verb phrase | Махнути рукою, помахати | To move your hand as a greeting or goodbye | She gave me a wave from across the street. |
| 5 | **grimace** | Verb / Noun | Скривитися; гримаса | To twist your face in an expression of pain, disgust, or disapproval; an ugly twisted expression | He grimaced in pain when he tried to stand up. |
| 6 | **grip** | Verb / Noun | Стискати, хапати; захват, хватка | To hold something tightly; a tight hold on something | She gripped the rope tightly as she climbed. |
| 7 | **hesitate** | Verb | Вагатися, коливатися | To pause before doing something, often because you are uncertain or nervous | Don't hesitate to ask if you need help. |
| 8 | **let out a groan** | Verb phrase | Застогнати, видати стогін | To make a deep sound expressing pain, disappointment, or annoyance | He let out a groan when he saw how much homework he had. |
| 9 | **manipulate** | Verb | Маніпулювати, керувати, контролювати | To control or influence someone cleverly or unfairly; to handle or control something skillfully | She tried to manipulate him into doing what she wanted. |
| 10 | **swallow** | Verb | Ковтати, проковтувати | To cause food or drink to pass down your throat; to accept something unpleasant | She swallowed nervously before giving her presentation. |

## Verbs of Looking

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 11 | **gaze** | Verb / Noun | Дивитися (пильно, тривалий час); погляд | To look steadily at something for a long time; a steady look | She gazed out of the window, lost in thought. |
| 12 | **glance** | Verb / Noun | Глянути, кинути погляд; короткий погляд | To look at something quickly; a quick look | He glanced at his watch to check the time. |
| 13 | **glimpse** | Verb / Noun | Побачити мельком; мигтіння, короткий вигляд | To see something or someone for a very short time; a brief or incomplete view | I caught a glimpse of her face before she disappeared into the crowd. |
| 14 | **peep** | Verb / Noun | Зазирнути, підглядати; короткий погляд | To look quickly and secretly, often through a small opening; a quick secret look | She peeped through the keyhole to see who was there. |
| 15 | **peer** | Verb | Вдивлятися, пильно дивитися | To look closely or with difficulty, especially because you cannot see well | He peered into the darkness, trying to see what was there. |
| 16 | **stare** | Verb / Noun | Витріщатися, дивитися не відриваючись; пильний погляд | To look at someone or something for a long time with your eyes wide open (often rude) | It's rude to stare at people. |
