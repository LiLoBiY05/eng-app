# Urban Life & City

Vocabulary for describing cities, streets, transport, and urban issues.

## Streets & Areas

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **cobbled streets** | Noun phrase | Вулиці, вимощені бруківкою | Streets paved with rounded stones (cobblestones) | All cobbled streets should be replaced by smooth modern surfaces like tarmac. |
| 2 | **tarmac** | Noun | Асфальт, тармак | A dark material used for making road surfaces (short for tarmacadam) | The old cobblestones were replaced with tarmac to make the road smoother. |
| 3 | **pedestrian zone** | Noun phrase | Пішохідна зона | An area in a town centre where vehicles are not allowed | Pedestrian zones should be for people on foot only — no cycling! |
| 4 | **backstreets** | Noun (plural) | Провулки, задвірки, глухі вулички | Small, quiet streets away from the main roads, often in poorer or less-known areas | We found a great little café hidden in the backstreets of the old town. |
| 5 | **neighbourhood** | Noun | Район, квартал, сусідство | An area of a town or city where people live; the people living in that area | It's a friendly neighbourhood — everyone knows each other. |
| 6 | **housing estate** | Noun phrase | Житловий масив, мікрорайон | A large group of houses or flats built together as a planned development | She grew up on a housing estate on the outskirts of the city. |
| 7 | **slum** | Noun | Нетрі, трущоби | A poor, overcrowded area of a city with bad living conditions | Many families living in the slums lack access to clean water. |
| 8 | **fast-flowing river** | Noun phrase | Швидкоплинна річка | A river where the water moves quickly | The fast-flowing river made it dangerous to swim near the bridge. |
| 9 | **canal** | Noun | Канал (штучний водний шлях) | An artificial waterway built for boats or irrigation | Narrowboats travel slowly along the canal through the city centre. |

## Transport & Parking

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 10 | **on-street parking** | Noun phrase | Парковка на вулиці (вздовж дороги) | Parking spaces located along the side of a road | On-street parking should be limited to residents only. |
| 11 | **limited to** | Verb phrase | Обмежений, доступний лише для | Restricted to a particular group or amount | Access to the gym is limited to members only. |
| 12 | **on foot** | Adverb phrase | Пішки | Walking, not using a vehicle | It takes about 20 minutes to get there on foot. |
| 13 | **underground car park** | Noun phrase | Підземний паркінг | A car park built below ground level | There should be more underground car parks to take cars off the street. |
| 14 | **take cars off the street** | Verb phrase | Прибрати автомобілі з вулиці | To reduce the number of vehicles parked or driving on public roads | Building more garages would help take cars off the street. |
| 15 | **cycle lane** | Noun phrase | Велосипедна смуга | A part of a road marked for use by cyclists only | There should be a lot more cycle lanes to encourage people to use bikes. |
| 16 | **encourage** | Verb | Заохочувати, спонукати | To give support or hope to someone; to make something more likely to happen | The city introduced free bus passes to encourage people to use public transport. |

## Issues & Describing Cities

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 17 | **noise pollution** | Noun phrase | Шумове забруднення | Harmful or excessive levels of noise in the environment | Anybody causing noise pollution should be heavily fined. |
| 18 | **heavily fined** | Verb phrase | Суворо оштрафований | Required to pay a large financial penalty | Drivers who park illegally in the city centre are heavily fined. |
| 19 | **get rid of** | Verb phrase | Позбутися, усунути | To remove or throw away something you no longer want or need | The city finally got rid of the old tram lines to ease traffic congestion. |
| 20 | **quaint** | Adjective | Затишний, старовинний, своєрідний | Attractively unusual or old-fashioned | The village was full of quaint cobbled streets and charming little shops. |
| 21 | **bustling** | Adjective | Жвавий, багатолюдний, метушливий | Full of busy activity and movement | The market was bustling with shoppers on Saturday morning. |
| 22 | **shabby** | Adjective | Занедбаний, потертий, обшарпаний | In poor condition through long use or lack of care | The building looked shabby — the paint was peeling and the windows were cracked. |
| 23 | **vibrant** | Adjective | Яскравий, жвавий, динамічний | Full of energy, life, and excitement | The city has a vibrant street art scene and a lively nightlife. |
