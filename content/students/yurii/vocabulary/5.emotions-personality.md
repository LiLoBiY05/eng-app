# Emotions & Personality

Vocabulary for describing feelings and character traits.

## Feelings & Emotions

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **confused** | Adjective | Розгублений, збентежений | Unable to understand or think clearly | I was confused by the instructions — they weren't clear. |
| 2 | **determined** | Adjective | Рішучий, цілеспрямований | Having made a firm decision and not changing it | She was determined to pass her exams no matter what. |
| 3 | **enthusiastic** | Adjective | Ентузіастичний, захоплений | Showing great interest and energy | The students were enthusiastic about the new project. |
| 4 | **relieved** | Adjective | Полегшений, заспокоєний | Feeling happy because something bad didn't happen | I was relieved when I found out I had passed. |
| 5 | **thrilled** | Adjective | У захваті, дуже радий | Very excited and happy | She was thrilled to get accepted into the programme. |
| 6 | **grief** | Noun | Горе, смуток, туга | Deep sadness, especially caused by someone's death | She was overcome with grief when her grandmother died. |

## Personality Traits

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 7 | **thorough** | Adjective | Ретельний, старанний, досконалий | Done carefully and completely, paying attention to every detail | She did a thorough job cleaning the house — every corner was spotless. |
| 8 | **witty** | Adjective | Дотепний, з почуттям гумору | Able to say clever and funny things quickly | He's very witty — he always makes everyone laugh with his quick remarks. |
| 9 | **affectionate** | Adjective | Ніжний, лагідний, люблячий | Showing feelings of love and care | She's very affectionate with her children — always hugging and kissing them. |
| 10 | **moody** | Adjective | Примхливий, мінливий у настрої | Having moods that change quickly and often; temperamental | He can be quite moody — happy one minute and upset the next. |
| 11 | **perceptive** | Adjective | Проникливий, спостережливий, тямущий | Good at noticing and understanding things quickly | She's very perceptive — she noticed I was upset even though I tried to hide it. |
| 12 | **spontaneous** | Adjective | Спонтанний, імпульсивний, непередбачуваний | Done or happening naturally without planning | She's very spontaneous — she just decided to book a flight to Paris on a whim. |
| 13 | **insecure** | Adjective | Невпевнений (у собі), незахищений | Lacking confidence; not feeling safe or protected | He feels insecure about his English skills even though he's quite good. |
| 14 | **daring** | Adjective | Сміливий, відважний, зухвалий | Brave and willing to take risks | She's very daring — she loves extreme sports like skydiving. |
| 15 | **tactful** | Adjective | Тактовний, делікатний | Careful not to say or do things that will upset people | He was very tactful when he told her the bad news — he chose his words carefully. |
| 16 | **self-centred** | Adjective | Егоцентричний, егоїстичний | Thinking only about yourself and not caring about others | He's very self-centred — he never asks how other people are feeling. |
| 17 | **self-conscious** | Adjective | Незручний, сором'язливий, скутий | Nervous or uncomfortable about your appearance or what others think of you | She felt self-conscious about speaking English in front of native speakers. |
| 18 | **self-critical** | Adjective | Самокритичний | Criticizing yourself and your own actions too much | She's too self-critical — she never gives herself credit for her achievements. |
| 19 | **good-natured** | Adjective | Добродушний, доброзичливий | Kind, friendly, and pleasant | He's very good-natured — he never gets angry, even when things go wrong. |
| 20 | **laid-back** | Adjective | Спокійний, розслаблений, невимушений | Relaxed and calm; not worried or stressed | My boss is very laid-back — she doesn't mind if we're a few minutes late. |
| 21 | **short-tempered** | Adjective | Запальний, дратівливий | Easily getting angry | He's quite short-tempered in the morning before he's had his coffee. |
| 22 | **strong-willed** | Adjective | Вольовий, цілеспрямований, впертий | Determined to do what you want, even if others disagree | She's very strong-willed — once she makes a decision, nothing can change her mind. |
| 23 | **level-headed** | Adjective | Розсудливий, тверезомислячий | Calm and sensible; making good decisions in difficult situations | We need a level-headed person to handle this crisis — someone who won't panic. |
| 24 | **fair-minded** | Adjective | Справедливий, неупереджений | Able to judge things fairly without being influenced by personal feelings | A good teacher should be fair-minded and treat all students equally. |
