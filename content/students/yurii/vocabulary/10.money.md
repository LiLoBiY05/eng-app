# Money & Shopping

Vocabulary related to budgeting, spending, shopping habits, and financial behaviour.

## Key Phrases

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **on a limited budget** | Prepositional phrase | З обмеженим бюджетом | Having only a small amount of money available to spend | It's hard to eat well when you're on a limited budget. |
| 2 | **birthday money** | Noun phrase | Гроші на день народження | Money received as a gift on your birthday | She spent her birthday money on a new pair of trainers. |
| 3 | **treat yourself (to sth)** | Verb phrase | Побалувати себе (чимось) | To buy or do something special for yourself as a reward or pleasure | After a tough week, I treated myself to a nice dinner. |
| 4 | **local producers** | Noun phrase | Місцеві виробники | Businesses, farmers, or makers that produce goods in your local area | Buying from local producers supports the community and reduces transport emissions. |
| 5 | **bargain-hunting** | Noun (gerund) | Полювання за вигідними пропозиціями | Searching for goods at the lowest possible price | She loves bargain-hunting in charity shops and markets. |
| 6 | **can't be bothered (to do sth)** | Verb phrase | Не маю бажання (робити щось) | To not want to make the effort to do something | I can't be bothered to cook tonight — let's order in. |
| 7 | **chain store** | Noun phrase | Мережевий магазин | A shop that is one of many owned by the same company | Most chain stores sell the same products regardless of location. |
| 8 | **reputation** | Noun | Репутація | The general opinion that people have about someone or something | The brand built its reputation on quality and fair pricing. |
| 9 | **cautious** | Adjective | Обережний | Careful to avoid danger, mistakes, or risk | Be cautious when using your card at unfamiliar ATMs. |
| 10 | **PIN** | Noun | ПІН-код | Personal Identification Number — a secret number used with a bank card | Never share your PIN with anyone, even bank staff. |

## Shopping & Spending Words

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **bargain** | Noun | Вигідна покупка, угода | Something bought for less than its usual price | That coat was a real bargain — it was half price! |
| 2 | **sales** | Noun (plural) | Розпродаж | A period when shops sell goods at reduced prices | I always wait for the January sales to buy new clothes. |
| 3 | **pick up** | Phrasal verb | Підхопити, знайти, купити | To buy or obtain something, often casually or at a bargain | I picked up a great jacket at the market for almost nothing. |
| 4 | **special offer** | Noun phrase | Спеціальна пропозиція | A product sold at a lower price than usual for a limited time | There's a special offer on coffee this week — buy two, get one free. |
| 5 | **charity shop** | Noun phrase | Магазин благодійної організації | A shop that sells second-hand goods to raise money for charity | You can find some amazing vintage items in charity shops. |
| 6 | **quality** | Noun / Adjective | Якість / Якісний | How good or well-made something is | It's worth paying more for quality that will last. |
| 7 | **last** | Verb | Тривати, бути міцним | To remain in good condition for a period of time | Good shoes will last for years if you look after them. |
| 8 | **fashion** | Noun | Мода | The style of clothes, behaviour, etc. that is popular at a particular time | She's never been interested in fashion — she wears what she likes. |
| 9 | **vintage** | Adjective / Noun | Вінтажний / Вінтаж | Old but high quality; clothing or items from a past era | He only wears vintage shirts from the 1960s and 70s. |
| 10 | **designer** | Adjective | Дизайнерський | Made by a famous fashion designer; high-end branded goods | Designer clothes are expensive, but some people buy them for the label. |
| 11 | **brand** | Noun | Бренд, марка | A name or logo that identifies a product or company | She buys things for the brand, not because they're better quality. |
| 12 | **reduced** | Adjective | Зі знижкою, зменшений у ціні | Sold at a lower price than normal | I always check the reduced section at the supermarket before I shop. |

## Shopping Collocations

| # | Collocation | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **mass-produced products** | Товари масового виробництва | Goods made in large quantities by machines, not by hand | Mass-produced products are cheaper but often lower in quality. |
| 2 | **bargain prices** | Вигідні ціни, ціни зі знижкою | Prices that are much lower than usual | You can find furniture at bargain prices at the end of the season. |
| 3 | **chain store** | Мережевий магазин | A shop that is one of many identical outlets owned by the same company | Most high streets are dominated by chain stores now. |
| 4 | **ethical values** | Етичні цінності | Beliefs about what is morally right and wrong, e.g. fairness, sustainability, honesty | Many consumers look for brands that share their ethical values. |
| 5 | **impulse purchases** | Імпульсивні покупки | Things you buy without planning to, often on a sudden feeling | Supermarkets place sweets near the checkout to encourage impulse purchases. |
| 6 | **local brands** | Місцеві бренди | Products made and sold by businesses in your local area | Supporting local brands helps the regional economy grow. |
| 7 | **online shopping** | Онлайн-шопінг | Buying goods or services via the internet | Online shopping has grown massively since the pandemic. |
| 8 | **responsible attitude** | Відповідальне ставлення | A sensible and trustworthy approach to something, such as spending and shopping | Would you say you have a responsible attitude to shopping? |

## Money Phrasal Verbs

| # | Word/Phrase | Part of Speech | Ukrainian | Definition | Example |
|---|-------------|----------------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | **pay out for (sth)** | Phrasal verb | Витрачати гроші на щось | To spend a large amount of money on something, often unwillingly | I had to pay out for a new boiler last month — it was so expensive. |
| 2 | **check up on (sb/sth)** | Phrasal verb | Перевіряти (когось/щось) | To find out information about someone or something to make sure they are correct or safe | I always check up on prices online before buying anything. |
| 3 | **shop around** | Phrasal verb | Порівнювати ціни, шукати вигідніший варіант | To compare prices in different shops before buying | It's worth shopping around before you buy a new phone. |
| 4 | **put aside** | Phrasal verb | Відкладати (гроші) | To save money regularly for future use | I try to put aside a small amount every month for emergencies. |
| 5 | **cut down on (sth)** | Phrasal verb | Скорочувати (витрати на щось) | To reduce the amount of money you spend on something | We need to cut down on eating out if we want to save money. |
| 6 | **look out for (sth)** | Phrasal verb | Стежити за (вигідними пропозиціями), бути напоготові | To watch or search for something useful or good | I always look out for deals and discounts when I'm shopping. |
| 7 | **send back** | Phrasal verb | Повертати (товар) | To return an item to the seller or sender | The shoes didn't fit, so I sent them back and got a refund. |
| 8 | **spring up** | Phrasal verb | З'являтися раптово, виникати | To appear or develop suddenly and quickly | New coffee shops have been springing up all over the city centre. |
