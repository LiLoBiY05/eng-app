# Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs are combinations of a verb + particle (preposition or adverb) that create a new meaning, often very different from the original verb.

**Example:** *give* (давати) + *up* = *give up* (здаватися, кидати щось)

## Types of Phrasal Verbs

### 1. Intransitive (неподільні без додатка)
No object needed:
> *The plane **took off** on time.*
> *Please **come along**!*

### 2. Separable (подільні)
Object can go between the verb and particle:
> *I **picked up** the book.* = *I **picked** the book **up**.*

⚠️ **With pronouns — always split:**
- ✅ *Pick **it** up.*
- ❌ *Pick up it.*

### 3. Inseparable (неподільні з додатком)
Object always comes after the particle:
> *I **ran into** an old friend.* (NOT: *ran an old friend into*)

### 4. Three-word phrasal verbs
Verb + two particles:
> *I **look forward to** the weekend.*
> *We need to **come up with** a plan.*

## How to Learn Phrasal Verbs

1. **Learn in context** — not just the translation, but full sentences
2. **Group by verb** — all phrasal verbs with *get*, then *take*, etc.
3. **Use flashcards** — phrasal verb on one side, example on the other
4. **5–7 words a day** — slow and steady is more effective
5. **Use them in conversation** — try to use new verbs immediately
6. **Watch series/films** — phrasal verbs appear constantly in natural speech

## Most Common Base Verbs

- **get** (get up, get along, get over, get by)
- **take** (take off, take in, take on, take up)
- **turn** (turn up, turn down, turn in, turn out)
- **come** (come along, come across, come up with)
- **go** (go away, go over, go on, go into)
- **look** (look up, look forward to, look after, look into, look up to)
- **put** (put off, put up with, put on, put away)
- **make** (make up, make up for, make for)
- **run** (run into, run out of, run over)
- **set** (set off, set out, set up)

---

## Key Rules

1. **Don't translate literally** — *give up* ≠ "давати вгору"
2. **One phrasal verb, many meanings** — always check context
3. **Write example sentences** — not just translations
4. **Mistakes are normal** — they're part of learning
